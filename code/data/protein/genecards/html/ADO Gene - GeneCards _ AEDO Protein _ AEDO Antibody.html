Summaries for ADO Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for ADO Gene

  • Human thiol dioxygenases include cysteine dioxygenase (CDO; MIM 603943) and cysteamine (2-aminoethanethiol) dioxygenase (ADO; EC 1.13.11.19). CDO adds 2 oxygen atoms to free cysteine, whereas ADO adds 2 oxygen atoms to free cysteamine to form hypotaurine (Dominy et al., 2007 [PubMed 17581819]).[supplied by OMIM, Mar 2008]

GeneCards Summary for ADO Gene

ADO (2-Aminoethanethiol (Cysteamine) Dioxygenase) is a Protein Coding gene. Diseases associated with ADO include acrodysostosis. Among its related pathways are Metabolism and Taurine and hypotaurine metabolism. GO annotations related to this gene include cysteamine dioxygenase activity.
