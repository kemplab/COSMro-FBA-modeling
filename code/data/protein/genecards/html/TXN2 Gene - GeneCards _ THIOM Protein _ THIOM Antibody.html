Summaries for TXN2 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for TXN2 Gene

  • This nuclear gene encodes a mitochondrial member of the thioredoxin family, a group of small multifunctional redox-active proteins. The encoded protein may play important roles in the regulation of the mitochondrial membrane potential and in protection against oxidant-induced apoptosis. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008]

GeneCards Summary for TXN2 Gene

TXN2 (Thioredoxin 2) is a Protein Coding gene. Diseases associated with TXN2 include penile cancer and ocular hypertension. Among its related pathways are Glucose / Energy Metabolism and MAPK Signaling: Oxidative Stress Pathway. GO annotations related to this gene include protein complex binding and oxidoreductase activity, acting on a sulfur group of donors, disulfide as acceptor.

UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot for TXN2 Gene

  • THIOM_HUMAN,Q99757

  • Has an anti-apoptotic function and plays an important role in the regulation of mitochondrial membrane potential. Could be involved in the resistance to anti-tumor agents. Possesses a dithiol-reducing activity.
