Summaries for ALDH9A1 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for ALDH9A1 Gene

  • This protein belongs to the aldehyde dehydrogenase family of proteins. It has a high activity for oxidation of gamma-aminobutyraldehyde and other amino aldehydes. The enzyme catalyzes the dehydrogenation of gamma-aminobutyraldehyde to gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA). This isozyme is a tetramer of identical 54-kD subunits. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008]

GeneCards Summary for ALDH9A1 Gene

ALDH9A1 (Aldehyde Dehydrogenase 9 Family Member A1) is a Protein Coding gene. Diseases associated with ALDH9A1 include alcohol dependence and fetal alcohol spectrum disorder. Among its related pathways are Arginine and proline metabolism and Porphyrin and chlorophyll metabolism. GO annotations related to this gene include protein homodimerization activity and NAD binding. An important paralog of this gene is ALDH1A2.
