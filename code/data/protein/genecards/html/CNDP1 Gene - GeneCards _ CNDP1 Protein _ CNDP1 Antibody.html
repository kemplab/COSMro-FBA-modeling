Summaries for CNDP1 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for CNDP1 Gene

  • This gene encodes a member of the M20 metalloprotease family. The encoded protein is specifically expressed in the brain, is a homodimeric dipeptidase which was identified as human carnosinase. This gene contains trinucleotide (CTG) repeat length polymorphism in the coding region. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008]

GeneCards Summary for CNDP1 Gene

CNDP1 (Carnosine Dipeptidase 1 (Metallopeptidase M20 Family)) is a Protein Coding gene. Diseases associated with CNDP1 include homocarnosinosis and microvascular complications of diabetes 3. Among its related pathways are Arginine and proline metabolism and beta-Alanine metabolism (KEGG). GO annotations related to this gene include hydrolase activity and carboxypeptidase activity. An important paralog of this gene is CNDP2.

Q96KN2-CNDP1_HUMAN
The number of trinucleotide (CTG) repeat varies among different alleles leading to insertion of Leu residues in the signal peptide. The allele with 5 leucines (as shown in the reference entry) is known as the Mannheim allele. Diabetic patients with the CNDP1 Mannheim allele are less susceptible for nephropathy.
