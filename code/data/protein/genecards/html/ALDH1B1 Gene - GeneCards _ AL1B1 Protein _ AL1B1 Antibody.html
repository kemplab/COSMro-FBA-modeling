Summaries for ALDH1B1 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for ALDH1B1 Gene

  • This protein belongs to the aldehyde dehydrogenases family of proteins. Aldehyde dehydrogenase is the second enzyme of the major oxidative pathway of alcohol metabolism. This gene does not contain introns in the coding sequence. The variation of this locus may affect the development of alcohol-related problems. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008]

GeneCards Summary for ALDH1B1 Gene

ALDH1B1 (Aldehyde Dehydrogenase 1 Family Member B1) is a Protein Coding gene. Diseases associated with ALDH1B1 include cyclothymic disorder and succinic semialdehyde dehydrogenase deficiency. Among its related pathways are Arginine and proline metabolism and Porphyrin and chlorophyll metabolism. GO annotations related to this gene include oxidoreductase activity and aldehyde dehydrogenase (NAD) activity. An important paralog of this gene is ALDH1A2.

UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot for ALDH1B1 Gene

  • AL1B1_HUMAN,P30837

  • ALDHs play a major role in the detoxification of alcohol-derived acetaldehyde. They are involved in the metabolism of corticosteroids, biogenic amines, neurotransmitters, and lipid peroxidation.
