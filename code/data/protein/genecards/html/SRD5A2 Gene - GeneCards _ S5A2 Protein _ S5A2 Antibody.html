Summaries for SRD5A2 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for SRD5A2 Gene

  • This gene encodes a microsomal protein expressed at high levels in androgen-sensitive tissues such as the prostate. The encoded protein is active at acidic pH and is sensitive to the 4-azasteroid inhibitor finasteride. Deficiencies in this gene can result in male pseudohermaphroditism, specifically pseudovaginal perineoscrotal hypospadias (PPSH). [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008]

GeneCards Summary for SRD5A2 Gene

SRD5A2 (Steroid 5 Alpha-Reductase 2) is a Protein Coding gene. Diseases associated with SRD5A2 include pseudovaginal perineoscrotal hypospadias and steroid 5-alpha-reductase deficiency. Among its related pathways are Glioma and Steroid hormone biosynthesis. GO annotations related to this gene include oxidoreductase activity, acting on the CH-CH group of donors and cholestenone 5-alpha-reductase activity. An important paralog of this gene is SRD5A1.

UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot for SRD5A2 Gene

  • S5A2_HUMAN,P31213

  • Converts testosterone (T) into 5-alpha-dihydrotestosterone (DHT) and progesterone or corticosterone into their corresponding 5-alpha-3-oxosteroids. It plays a central role in sexual differentiation and androgen physiology.
