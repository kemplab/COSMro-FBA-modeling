Summaries for AGPAT4 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for AGPAT4 Gene

  • This gene encodes a member of the 1-acylglycerol-3-phosphate O-acyltransferase family. This integral membrane protein converts lysophosphatidic acid to phosphatidic acid, the second step in de novo phospholipid biosynthesis. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008]

GeneCards Summary for AGPAT4 Gene

AGPAT4 (1-Acylglycerol-3-Phosphate O-Acyltransferase 4) is a Protein Coding gene. Among its related pathways are triacylglycerol biosynthesis and Glycerophospholipid biosynthesis. GO annotations related to this gene include transferase activity, transferring acyl groups and 1-acylglycerol-3-phosphate O-acyltransferase activity. An important paralog of this gene is LCLAT1.

UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot for AGPAT4 Gene

  • PLCD_HUMAN,Q9NRZ5

  • Converts lysophosphatidic acid (LPA) into phosphatidic acid by incorporating an acyl moiety at the sn-2 position of the glycerol backbone.
