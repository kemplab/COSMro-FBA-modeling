Summaries for ETNK2 Gene

Entrez Gene Summary for ETNK2 Gene

  • The protein encoded by this gene is a member of choline/ethanolamine kinase family which catalyzes the first step of phosphatidylethanolamine (PtdEtn) biosynthesis via the cytidine diphosphate (CDP) ethanolamine pathway. Alternative splicing results in multiple transcript variants. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2014]

GeneCards Summary for ETNK2 Gene

ETNK2 (Ethanolamine Kinase 2) is a Protein Coding gene. Among its related pathways are Glycerophospholipid biosynthesis and Metabolism. GO annotations related to this gene include transferase activity, transferring phosphorus-containing groups and ethanolamine kinase activity. An important paralog of this gene is CHKB.

UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot for ETNK2 Gene

  • EKI2_HUMAN,Q9NVF9

  • Highly specific for ethanolamine phosphorylation. Does not have choline kinase activity (By similarity).
